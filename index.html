Projects

  • Transfer Learning in Reinforcement Learning

    In this project, we investigate the problem of transferring the experiences of a RL agent across different environments in continuous control (for example, an agent should learn to play a 2kg pendulum rapidly after learning how to play a 1kg pendulum). We found that a state value based method consistently performs better than policy gradient based methods, and we tried to find an explanation from an optimization perspective.

  • Improving Visual Object Tracking with Saliency Algorithms

    Correlation filters (CFs) are fundamental components in most of the modern visual object tracking algorithms. We found that the cosine mask, which is used to mitigate the influence of the background, may cause unrecoverable drifting. However, removing this mask also catastrophically hurts the performance of the trackers. To suppress its side effect, We combined the saliency algorithms with several CF trackers (both traditional and deep), and obtained better results on OTB100 & UAV123.
